The system must ensure that any request for access to protected health information is acted upon within 30 days of receipt, either by granting access or issuing a written denial.
The system must ensure that if access is granted, in whole or in part, the individual is notified of the acceptance and provided with the requested access.
The system must provide the requested access in the form and format specified by the individual, if readily producible. If it is not readily producible, the information must be provided in a readable hard copy or another format agreed upon by the individual and the covered entity.
The system must provide access in the electronic form and format requested by the individual if the information is maintained electronically and it is readily producible. If it is not readily producible in the requested format, it must be provided in another readable electronic format agreed upon by the individual and the covered entity.
The system may provide a summary or explanation of the requested protected health information instead of full access, only if the individual agrees to receive a summary or explanation in advance and agrees to any fees associated with it.
The system must ensure that access to protected health information is provided in a timely manner, including offering to arrange a convenient time and place for inspection or mailing the information if requested. The system must also support discussions with the individual to clarify scope, format, or other access-related preferences.
The system must support an individual's request to transmit a copy of their protected health information directly to another person, provided the request is in writing, signed by the individual, and clearly identifies the recipient and the delivery location.
The system may allow the imposition of a reasonable, cost-based fee for copies, summaries, or explanations of protected health information. The fee must include only the cost of:Labor for copying the information in paper or electronic form, Supplies for creating the copy (e.g., paper or portable media), Postage, if the individual requests mailed delivery, and Preparation of a summary or explanation, if agreed to in advance.
The system must allow partial access to any requested protected health information not subject to denial, even if part of the request is denied.
The system must provide a timely, written denial in plain language when access is denied. The denial must include: The reason for the denial, A statement of the individual's right to request a review, if applicable, and how to exercise that right, and Instructions on how to file a complaint, including the name or title and telephone number of a designated contact person.
If the system does not maintain the requested protected health information but has knowledge of where it is maintained, it must inform the individual where to direct their access request.
If an individual requests a review of a denied access request, the system must ensure the request is referred to a licensed health care professional who was not involved in the original denial. The reviewing official must issue a written decision within a reasonable time based on the applicable denial standards, and the system must act according to that decision.
The system must maintain documentation of: The designated record sets subject to individual access rights, and The titles of personnel or offices responsible for receiving and handling such access requests.
§ 164.526 Amendment of protected health information.
The system must allow individuals to request an amendment to their protected health information maintained in designated record sets, for as long as the information is retained in those sets.
The system must allow the covered entity to deny an amendment request if: The information was not created by the entity, unless the originator is no longer available to amend it. The information is not part of the designated record set. The information is not eligible for access under the access provision. The information is already accurate and complete.
The system may require that amendment requests be submitted in writing and include a reason for the amendment, as long as individuals are informed of these requirements in advance.
The system must ensure that any request for amendment to protected health information is acted upon within 60 calendar days of receipt.
If the covered entity cannot act within 60 days, the system must allow one extension of up to 30 days, provided that: A written notice is sent to the individual within the original 60-day period, The notice includes the reason for the delay and a specific completion date.
When an amendment is accepted, the system must: Identify the affected records within the designated record set, Append or link the amendment to the original record.
Upon accepting an amendment, the system must: Notify the individual in a timely manner, Obtain the individual’s agreement on who should be informed of the amendment.
The system must allow the covered entity to make reasonable efforts to share accepted amendments with: Individuals/entities identified by the patient, Any third parties (including business associates) known to hold or rely on the original data.
If an amendment request is denied, the system must generate a written denial that includes: The reason for denial, The individual's right to submit a statement of disagreement, The right to request future disclosures include the amendment request and denial, How to file a complaint with contact information.
The system must allow individuals to submit a written statement of disagreement and optionally allow covered entities to limit the length of such statements.
The system must allow the covered entity to issue a written rebuttal to the individual’s disagreement and ensure a copy is shared with the individual.
The system must ensure that all documents related to denied amendments (request, denial, disagreement, rebuttal) are: Appended or linked to the relevant protected health information record in the designated record set.
If an individual has submitted a statement of disagreement, the system must ensure that: The amendment request, denial, and disagreement (or a summary thereof) are included with all future disclosures of the relevant protected health information.
If no statement of disagreement is submitted, but the individual has requested inclusion, the system must include: The amendment request and denial (or a summary thereof) in all future disclosures of the relevant information.
If the system uses a standard transaction format (per 45 CFR Part 162) that does not support attachment of amendment-related materials, the system must: Transmit the required amendment materials separately to the recipient of the standard transaction.
When notified by another covered entity of an amendment to protected health information, the system must: Apply the amendment to the designated record set in accordance with internal amendment procedures.
The system must record and retain The titles of persons or offices responsible for receiving and processing amendment requests
